Book List for ENGL 491

The following list of books are what was required for the course.


The following list are books and materials that I added to my personal reading,
as the topics and themes that appeared over the course of the semester
enticed me to do further reading and learning beyond the course. (Non-exhaustive)

Materials on Education and Teaching Methods
A majority of these books I found either by researching more work by theorists and resources found in Beyond English by Milner, et al. that I enjoyed or particularly favored in the assigned readings, or I found by finding more reading or materials on aspects or methods of teaching that I wanted to solidify or strengthen beyond the course.
  • Teaching When the World is on Fire by Lisa Delpit
  • Grading Smarter, Not Harder by Myron Dueck
  • Differentiation by Rick Wormeli
  • Teaching about Race and Racism in the Classroom by Cyndi Kernahan
  • So You Want to Learn about Race by Ijeoma Oluo
Materials about or by Authors of Color
The following books caught my interest as I came across them, as I found the reading of All American Boys and The Poet X to be extremely powerful and enjoyable.
  • The Water Dancer by Ta-Nehisi Coates
  • Stamped: Racism, antiracism, and you by Jason Reynolds and Dr. Ibram X. Kendi
  • Four-Hundred Souls by Dr. Ibram X. Kendi
  • Dear Martin by Nic Stone
  • Black Girl, Call Home by Jasmine Mans
Materials on LGBTQ+
This was list was brought on because I and another LGBTQ+ classmate were unimpressed with Beyond Magenta and how poorly it discussed and introduced pre-service teachers to trans issues and preparing them to have LGBTQ+ students in their classroom. We eventually stated our case to the professor to retire the book from the class. In the hope to finding something better, I did some searching for better LGBTQ+ literature that would be beneficial for not only pre-service teachers but within my own classroom library.
  • A Year without a Name: A Memoir by Cyrus Dunham
  • Call Me By Your Name by André Aciman
  • What if it's Us by Becky Albertalli
  • This is how it Always is by Laurie Frankel
  • The 57 Bus: A true story of two teenagers and the crime that changed their lives by Dashka Slater
  • Leaving isn't the Hardest Thing: Essays by Lauren Hough
  • This is Where it Ends by Marieke Nijkamp
  • Paper Bullets: Two artists who risked their lives to defy the nazis by Jeffrey Jackson
