Notes

  • Keep in mind your students' worry, ability, and grade level when assigning a speech and the speech length.
  • Keep the speech relaxed. No Big Deal. Speeches can be dreadful to some.
  • Oral citations: where and when source was published.

Formal Outline for Speeches:

  • Introduction: *Most/all of the introduction will be written on here within the outline.
    • Attention-Getter: (Do not say "My topic is...")
    • Listener Relevance/Significance: (Why should we care?)
    • Thesis Statement: (One sentence statement of topic)
    • Specific Preview: (Specific list of your 2-4 main points)
  • Body of Speech: *Written as an outline
    • Main point: Two - three main points listed as complete sentences.
      1. (At least two supporting points for each main point.)

    Transition:

    1. Main point
      1. Supporting point
      2. Supporting point

    Transition:

  • Conclusion
    • Summary: (A specific list reviewing your main points)
    • Clincher: (A closing statement linked back to your intro if possible. Not "Thank you.")

    References (APA) or Works Cited (MLA)
